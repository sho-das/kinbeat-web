Delete your KinBeat account

For the KinBeat app by Kinso Labs Limited

This page explains how to delete your KinBeat account and all of your associated data, or how to ask us to delete only some of your data. KinBeat is made by Kinso Labs Limited, a company registered in England and Wales.

Option 1: Delete from inside the app

  1. Open the KinBeat app and sign in.
  2. Go to Settings.
  3. Tap Delete account.
  4. Confirm.

You then have a 14-day grace period. During those 14 days you are signed out, but you can sign back in with the same account to restore everything. After 14 days, your account and all your data are permanently removed.

Option 2: Request deletion by email

If you no longer have the app installed, or you would rather we do it for you, email us and we will delete your account and all associated data.

Email us to delete your account

Please send the request from the email address on your KinBeat account so we can verify it is you. We action deletion requests within 30 days. Our contact address is contact@kinbeat.com.

Delete only some of your data

If you want to remove specific data without closing your account - for example, your phone number or a family link - you can:

  • Edit or clear it inside the app (for example, remove a family member, or clear your nickname or phone number in Settings), or
  • Email contact@kinbeat.com describing what you would like removed.

Email us to delete some of your data

What is deleted, and what is kept

When you delete your account, we remove:

  • Your account details - email address, name, language and role.
  • Your family links and any nicknames you set.
  • Your push notification token.
  • Your daily activity summaries.

Retention periods:

  • Account and personal data - fully purged within 30 days of your deletion request (after the 14-day grace period, if you delete from inside the app).
  • Daily activity summaries on our servers - kept for 7 days in normal use, then automatically deleted.
  • Data of someone who leaves a family - kept for 7 days after they leave, then deleted.
  • We may keep a minimal record only where the law requires it (for example, to meet a legal or accounting obligation), and only for as long as required.

We do not sell your data and we do not share it with advertisers or data brokers. For full details, see our Privacy Policy.
