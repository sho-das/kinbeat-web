Questions families ask us

How does KinBeat detect that my parent is unwell?

KinBeat uses the phone's built-in motion sensor — the same one inside every modern smartphone. If the phone stays completely still for longer than the time you set, every family member is alerted. There are no cameras, no microphones, and no GPS tracking.

Does my parent need a smartphone?

Yes. KinBeat runs on the parent's Android phone. The phone needs to be carried in a pocket, on a belt clip, or on the person — that is what gives the motion signal. iPhone is supported for family members watching the parent.

Is this another tracking app?

No. KinBeat is the opposite of a tracking app. We do not collect location, browsing history, photos, or audio. We watch one signal — movement — and only act when something looks wrong.

What happens at night when my parent is sleeping?

You set a sleep window — for example 10 PM to 7 AM. During that time, KinBeat does not raise alerts. As soon as the window ends, watching resumes automatically.

What if my parent's phone runs out of battery?

The family will see the parent's phone as offline and silent — and KinBeat tells the family clearly that the alert system is paused. There is no false silence: if KinBeat cannot watch, you know.

How much does KinBeat cost?

KinBeat starts with a 14-day free trial. After that it is £2.99 / ₹99 a month for the family, plus £1 / ₹49 for each additional parent you watch. One subscription covers every family member with the app installed.

Which languages does KinBeat support?

KinBeat speaks 22 languages: English, Hindi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Gujarati, Kannada, Malayalam, Punjabi, Urdu, Odia, Sinhala, Spanish, French, Indonesian, Filipino, Vietnamese, Thai, Japanese, Korean and Arabic. The brand name KinBeat stays in English in every language.

Is the app available on iPhone?

The family-monitoring side of KinBeat works on iPhone. The parent (the person being watched) needs to be on Android, because the motion-sensor work runs reliably on Android in the background. We are working on a parent-side iOS build.
